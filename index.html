Whats is ABV?

Alcohol by volume is a standard measure of how much alcohol is contained in a given volume of an alcoholic beverage. It is defined as the number of millilitres of pure ethanol present in 100 mL of solution at 20 Â°C. The ABV standard is used worldwide.

What is IBU?

The IBU's in beer refers to the International Bitterness Unit scale which is a standard for measuring the amount of hops in one's beer. For example, a hoppy beer like an IPA will have a very high IBU rating, like a 75, while a malty beer like a stout will usually (but not always) have a low IBU rating at around a 30.
